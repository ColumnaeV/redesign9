CLSN - ART

There are many reasons why people travel vast distances to see the masterpieces of the world in the grand constructions such as the Louvre. The main reason I focused in on was inspiration. Most, if not all artists, create artwork to inspire, invoke emotions, or cause reflection within viewer: something which I hope my artwork can do. Previously its own subdomain, CLSN - ART was my own digital Louvre. Here you can find my works seeded by various sources of inspiration. Put into collections and ordered by most recent, you can now follow the changes and style in my journey of digital art.

What's On Display

Pieces. That's what is on display. That's what I call all my works of art. Although they're all pieces, you may notice that all collections contain a print count. Prints are usually distinguishable from the rest (not really now) as they are always 300 dpi, the minimum standard dpi count for physical printing. What makes them unique are the labels in which usually can be found in the bottom left corner. The original source is either myself or I have received permission from the original photographer to do a publicised edit of it. Reasoning for this is that these pieces have been entered in art competitions.

Outlining Life

June 2019 — Digital Art Exhibit
0 Prints
feat. Christopher He

As the title states, all the works found in this collection are outlines of a subject whilst demonstrating life. This exhibit began as my replication of an edit found on Disclosure's album artwork. Using a white coloured chalk outline, the subject is enhanced through various edits of the original image as a background.
